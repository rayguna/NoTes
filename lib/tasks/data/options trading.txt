Call Option;A call option gives the holder the right to buy an asset at a specified price within a specific time period.
Put Option;A put option gives the holder the right to sell an asset at a specified price within a specific time period.
Strike Price;The strike price is the set price at which an option can be bought or sold when exercised.
Expiration Date;The expiration date is the date on which an option expires and can no longer be exercised.
Premium;The premium is the price paid for purchasing an option.
In the Money;An option is in the money if exercising it would result in a profit.
Out of the Money;An option is out of the money if exercising it would not result in a profit.
At the Money;An option is at the money if the strike price is equal to the current price of the underlying asset.
Covered Call;A covered call involves holding a long position in an asset while selling a call option on the same asset.
Naked Call;A naked call involves selling a call option without owning the underlying asset.
Protective Put;A protective put involves buying a put option for an asset already owned to hedge against potential losses.
Long Straddle;A long straddle involves buying both a call and a put option at the same strike price and expiration date.
Iron Condor;An iron condor is a strategy that involves holding a combination of four different options with different strike prices.
Volatility;Volatility measures the price fluctuations of an asset.
Delta;Delta measures the sensitivity of an option's price to changes in the price of the underlying asset.
Gamma;Gamma measures the rate of change of delta over time.
Theta;Theta measures the rate of decline in the value of an option due to the passage of time.
Vega;Vega measures the sensitivity of an option's price to changes in the volatility of the underlying asset.
Rho;Rho measures the sensitivity of an option's price to changes in interest rates.
Implied Volatility;Implied volatility reflects the market's view of the likelihood of changes in an asset's price.
Exercise;Exercise is the act of utilizing the right to buy or sell the underlying asset of an option.
Assignment;Assignment occurs when the seller of an option is obligated to fulfill the terms of the option contract.
Margin Requirement;Margin requirement is the minimum amount of equity that must be maintained in a margin account.
Hedging;Hedging is the practice of taking a position in one market to offset potential losses in another.
Speculation;Speculation involves trading with the expectation of making a profit from market movements.
